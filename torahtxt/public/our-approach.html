Our Approach

Where the messages come from, how they're written, who they're for — and when we go quiet.

📖 What We Send

Every morning, TorahTxt delivers a short D'var Torah — a Torah teaching — grounded in the weekly parasha (Torah portion) and connected to something real in daily life. The goal is never to lecture. It's to spark a thought you'll carry through your day.

Messages are approximately 300 characters for SMS, with a fuller version available via email subscription.

Weekly rhythm

  • Sunday – Monday: Introduce the main theme of the weekly parasha
  • Tuesday – Thursday: Deeper dive — lesser-known characters, Talmudic or Chassidic angles, or a verse that doesn't get enough attention
  • Friday: Shabbat readiness — reflection, preparation, or a Shabbat-specific teaching tied to the parasha

On weeks where the parasha theme has been thoroughly covered, we pivot to Talmud lessons, Pirkei Avot, or Chassidic stories — always thematically connected when possible.

📚 Sources We Draw From

Every TorahTxt message is grounded in traditional Jewish texts. We draw from:

Torah & Tanakh
The Five Books of Moses and the broader Hebrew Bible — the foundation of every teaching
Talmud (Bavli & Yerushalmi)
The oral law, rabbinic debates, and stories that illuminate the written Torah
Midrash
Rabbinic narrative and homiletic literature that fills in the spaces between the verses
Rashi (1040–1105)
The foundational medieval commentator — concise, textually precise, indispensable
Rambam / Maimonides (1138–1204)
Philosopher, legal codifier, and rationalist — a bridge between Torah and universal human reason
Sefat Emet (Gerrer Rebbe, 1847–1905)
One of the greatest Chassidic commentators — dense, mystical, and profoundly relevant
Pirkei Avot
Ethics of the Fathers — timeless wisdom on character, relationships, and purpose
Chassidic Masters
Teachings from the Baal Shem Tov, Maggid of Mezeritch, Sfas Emes, and others who made Torah emotionally alive

We always aim to explain our sources in plain language — no jargon without context, no citations without meaning.

🕍 Theological Orientation

TorahTxt's messages are closest to Modern Orthodox / Centrist Orthodox in their framing — though we write for anyone who cares about their Judaism. Here's what that means in practice:

  • Every teaching is grounded in classical sources (Torah text, Talmud, Midrash, Rashi, Rambam)
  • We draw heavily from Chassidic masters — especially the Sefat Emet — which reflects a specifically Orthodox and spiritually engaged lens
  • We treat the parasha as authoritative Jewish text, not as myth or metaphor
  • We reference Shabbat, Pesach, and mitzvot as real obligations, not just cultural practices
  • The tone is warm and accessible — written like a wise friend, not an academic paper

Who it resonates with

✅
Modern Orthodox
Perfect fit. Serious Torah learning, emotionally engaged, accessible language.
✅
Traditional / Masorti Conservative
Works well — especially the Talmud and Midrash angles.

Our goal: speak from Hebrew school to beit midrash — reaching Jews across denominations who care about their Judaism, anchored in Orthodox tradition but written with a tone that never excludes.

🕯️ When We Go Quiet

TorahTxt observes Shabbat and major Jewish holidays. No messages are sent during these times — because we believe the medium should honor the message.

Shabbat

No messages are sent on Saturday. On weeks with a Friday message, we aim to deliver it before Shabbat begins (before sundown Friday). The silence of Shabbat is intentional — it's the rest the week builds toward.

Jewish Holidays

TorahTxt pauses on the following holidays (Yom Tov days). When a holiday falls, the day's message is skipped — not rescheduled.

Holiday When Days Off
Rosh Hashana1–2 Tishrei2 days
Yom Kippur10 Tishrei1 day
Sukkot (Yom Tov days)15–16 Tishrei + Shemini Atzeret/Simchat Torah4 days
Pesach (Yom Tov days)15–16 & 21–22 Nisan4 days
Shavuot6–7 Sivan2 days
Chanukah25 Kislev – 2/3 TevetNo pause (not Yom Tov)
Purim14 AdarNo pause (not Yom Tov)

Note: On Erev Pesach (the day before Passover) and other days with significant preparation, the message may be delivered early or skipped at our discretion.
